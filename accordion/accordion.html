Helpful information for Aetna® members on specialty medications

We know that understanding your specialty medication can feel overwhelming. Because you’re an Aetna health plan member, we want to help you make sense of it all.

Understand your grace fills

What’s a grace fill? If you’ve been getting your specialty medication from an out-of-network pharmacy, you may be allowed one or two fills from that pharmacy. But after those “grace fills” your medication may not be covered. You will need to switch to an in-network specialty pharmacy to continue to get your medication.

Use in-network specialty pharmacies to ensure coverage

We want to make sure you have access to your prescription benefits and receive your specialty medications quickly, conveniently, and on time. That’s why we want you to know about specialty network pharmacies. If you’re not sure if your pharmacy is in our network, check your Aetna member website to look up your specific plan benefits or call us at 1-800-872-3862 for support.

In the era of “social distancing,” choices in how you receive your medicines are valuable

Many pharmacies offer free delivery, so it’s good to consider your options.

Use a pillbox

What’s a grace fill? For oral medications that can be removed from their container or do not require special handling or storage, pillboxes with dedicated sections for days and time of day make it asier to remember to take your specialty medication. Keep your pillbox in a place where you will see it regularly, such as the kitchen table or your bathroom counter.

Combine with a daily task

Taking your specialty medication in conjunction with doing a daily task such as brushing your teeth, taking a shower, or eating breakfast can help you take your medication consistently every day. Just be sure to note whether there are specific directions to take your medication with or without food, or other administration recommendations.

Use a monthly calendar

Write down your daily specialty medication doses on a calendar at home or on a computer. Make sure to update it frequently and mark each dose you take.

Take advantage of technology

Smartphone apps can help you remember and track when you last took your specialty medications. You can get reminders to take your medication at a particular time or receive refill reminders directly on your mobile device.

Some specialty pharmacies offer secure digital tools that make it easier to manage your medications on your own time and stay on track, including:

  • Refilling and tracking orders
  • Choosing pickup or delivery
  • Setting email or text reminders
  • Viewing your prescription history

Get help from family, friends and other patients

Get support in reminding you to take your specialty medication. Some specialty pharmacies offer patient support programs based on your specialty condition. For example, by enrolling in an in-network specialty pharmacy, you can connect with other patients with your condition through MyHealthTeams, an online social network community of over 650,000 people living with chronic conditions. It offers networks for more than 24 conditions, including rheumatoid arthritis, multiple sclerosis, psoriasis, hemophilia and more.

Find out why your specialty medication is the best choice for you

No matter what your health condition or disease, there’s often more than one choice of medication. Ask why the medication your doctor has prescribed is the best choice for you.

Be sure you know how and when to take your specialty medicine

Morning or evening? Empty stomach or after a meal? Taking your specialty medicine correctly ensures that it gives you the help you need. Talk to your doctor to review the correct dose, time of day, frequency, and method to take your medication.

Review your specialty medicine side effects

All drugs have potential side effects, even Tylenol. Anyone can be allergic to a medicine, but certain symptoms are more serious. Whatever you experience is real, so make sure to keep a list or notes about your symptoms and share them with your doctor.

Tell your doctor about all medicine you’re taking

Sometimes, one medication may interact with other drugs, causing an adverse reaction. Talk to your doctor about all other medications and supplements that you’re taking so that they can advise you about possible interactions.
